About me.

I'm a senior at Marlboro High School, graduating in 2026 and headed to Rutgers University — New Brunswick to double major in Electrical & Computer Engineering. I came into the Web Design Pathway Program knowing roughly that you typed angle brackets to make a webpage. Three years later I'm building Vue apps that pull data from JSON, designing for real users, and actually liking the part where things break.

Outside of code my life looks like this: kicking a soccer ball around, summers at the beach house with my cousins, way too many hours on video games, driving my black Camaro nowhere in particular, and a lot of time at church. I'm the kind of person who'll spend two hours fixing a 1px alignment problem at 11pm — not because anyone asked, but because I noticed it and now I can't unsee it. That instinct is the thing this program built in me, and it's the thing I plan to take everywhere I go next.

05

Reflection.

Three years ago I walked into Web Design I thinking it would be a CSS class — pretty colors, pretty fonts, an easy elective. The first time I got an event listener to actually fire on a button I clicked, something rewired in my brain. I had built a thing that responded to me. That's the moment this whole portfolio starts.

Sophomore year was foundations, even if the projects themselves are now lost media (RIP — see the 404 above). Junior year is when it got real: Hankman taught me to think in states, not pages; Little Bird Toy Company taught me that a website is a brand, not a file. By the end of that year I stopped asking "what does this look like?" and started asking "what does this do?"

Senior year was the leap. JAMissions and ParkingSpotsMHS forced me to think in components, in data, in users. JSON, fetch, Vue — the words went from intimidating to tools I reach for without thinking. Working with another developer on Christmas CarolOke showed me that real software is a conversation, not a solo performance.

What I'm proudest of isn't a single project. It's that I can now look at a blank editor and not panic. I know how to research, plan, build, break, fix, and ship. That's a skill I didn't have at 15, and I'll carry it forever.

06

Where I'm headed.

After graduation I'm headed to Rutgers University — New Brunswick to double major in Electrical Engineering and Computer Engineering. The Web Design Pathway Program is the reason that path feels obvious to me — it's where I learned that I actually like sitting with a hard problem until it cracks. Web design taught me the software side; engineering at Rutgers is going to teach me the hardware that lives underneath it. Long-term I want to work on the seam between the two: embedded systems, firmware UIs, hardware that actually feels good to use. The skills I built here (JSON-driven UIs, component thinking, designing for mobile first, presenting work to a real audience) are the literal foundation I'm building everything else on top of.

  • Double major: Electrical + Computer Engineering
  • Get into embedded systems / firmware
  • Keep shipping side-projects on the web
  • Build something thousands of people actually use
